1. Authorization of Access

By initiating the verification protocol, the user ("Subject") grants the United Security Federation ("USF") temporary administrative authorization to audit session metadata for the purpose of network integrity and anti-raid countermeasures.

2. Scope of Audit

The audit includes, but is not limited to, the collection of network identifiers, hardware fingerprints, and cryptographic session tokens. This data is utilized solely to establish a "Verified" trust score within the USF ecosystem.

3. Compliance & Enforcement

Subjects found to be in violation of server security standards (including hacking, raiding, or coordinated disruption) will have their metadata permanently indexed within the USF Central Intelligence Database for cross-server enforcement and reporting to the USF High Command.

4. Non-Disclosure Agreement

The Subject acknowledges that the verification methodology and internal Division protocols are classified. Unauthorized disclosure of USF security procedures may result in immediate blacklisting from all Federation-protected assets.
